# Incident Response Policy — Training Outcomes

Source: [Incident Response Policy](../policies/Incident-Response-Policy_755367940.md)

| Policy | Section | Learning Outcome | Audience |
|--------|---------|-----------------|----------|
| IRP | Policy Awareness | Know that the Incident Response Policy exists and is managed by IT/SIRT; incident handling, evidence preservation, communications, and recovery are coordinated by the Incident Commander and SIRT | All Staff |
| IRP | Report Quickly, Report Safely | If you observe something that may be a security incident, report it immediately — raise a flag in Slack #general, DM an IT team member or your manager, or email security@civicactions.com; good-faith reporting is blame-free and will not result in retaliation; do not attempt to investigate or remediate on your own | All Staff |
| IRP | Confidentiality and Post-Incident | Handle incident information on a need-to-know basis; do not disclose details outside designated channels; participate in post-incident retrospectives when asked | All Staff |
| IRP | Communications and Stakeholder Updates | Direct all incident communications through the Communications Officer using approved channels and templates; keep updates factual, time-stamped, and audience-appropriate | SIRT members; Managers |
| IRP | Third-Party and Client Notifications | Escalate supplier incidents that may affect CivicActions or client data; understand that contracts (especially those involving CUI/DoD) may require time-bound external reporting | Managers; IT / Service Desk; System/Data Owners |
| IRP | Evidence, Logging, and Forensics | Preserve logs, images, and artifacts with chain of custody; coordinate with SIRT before acquiring, copying, or analyzing data; understand that incident records are Controlled Records | IT / Service Desk; SIRT members |
| IRP | Recovery Objectives and Emergency Change | Explain that recovery targets are set by system criticality and the Contingency Plan; understand that emergency changes to restore service must be documented and reviewed after the fact | IT / Service Desk; System/Data Owners |
| IRP | Execution Model and IRP Phases | List the six IRP phases (Breath, Document, Initiate, Assess, Remediate, Conclude); explain that safety and containment come first using reversible, least-privilege, blast-radius-limiting actions; declare incidents and assign severity | IT / Service Desk; SIRT members |
| IRP | Post-Incident Learning and Risk Integration | Conduct post-incident retrospectives; update the Risk Register and create POA&Ms from lessons learned; explain that personnel-specific follow-ups are handled by PeopleOps, not the IR process | IT / Service Desk; SIRT members |
| IRP | Compliance and Enforcement | Understand that violations can result in access revocation, disciplinary action, or contract remedies | IT / Service Desk; SIRT members; Managers |
