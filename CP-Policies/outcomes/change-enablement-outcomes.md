# Change Enablement Policy — Training Outcomes

Source: [Change Enablement Policy](../policies/Change-Enablement-Policy_768114689.md)

| Policy | Section | Learning Outcome | Audience |
|--------|---------|-----------------|----------|
| CEP | Change Classes and Criteria | Classify a production change as Standard (fully automated, pre-approved), Normal (peer-reviewed PR/MR, delegated approval), Significant (affects High Impact services, formal Change Record), or Emergency (Incident Commander authorized); apply the criteria to determine the correct class | IT / Service Desk; Developers; System/Data Owners |
| CEP | Required Content for Change Records | Complete a change record with all required fields: description, impacted services/CIs, impact, urgency, risk assessment, test results, implementation plan, change reason, communications plan, backout plan, test plan, maintenance window, and evidence | IT / Service Desk; Developers; System/Data Owners |
| CEP | Approvals and Guardrails | Explain that Normal changes use peer review + automated tests as primary approval; Significant changes require Change Authority approval and rollback plan; Emergency changes require Incident Commander approval with subsequent review | IT / Service Desk; Developers; System/Data Owners |
| CEP | Scheduling and Communication | Know that each user-facing service has a weekly maintenance window and participates in a monthly company calendar; understand that changes outside windows require justification and approval; notify stakeholders before and after impacting changes | IT / Service Desk; Developers; System/Data Owners |
| CEP | Post-Implementation Review | Verify Normal, Significant, and Emergency changes and update configuration records; conduct a retrospective within five business days when needed; capture lessons learned in the improvement register | IT / Service Desk; Developers; System/Data Owners |
| CEP | Change Freezes | Explain that change freezes may be applied during critical delivery periods or when error budgets are exceeded; only stability-improving fixes are permitted during a freeze | IT / Service Desk; Developers; System/Data Owners |
