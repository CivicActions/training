# Acceptable Use Policy — Training Outcomes

Source: [Acceptable Use Policy](../policies/Acceptable-Use-Policy_755957762.md)

| Policy | Section | Learning Outcome | Audience |
|--------|---------|-----------------|----------|
| AUP | Principles for Acceptable Use | State the core acceptable-use principles (approved devices, least privilege, authorized purpose, ask-before-bypass) and give an example of each | All Staff |
| AUP | Ownership, Privacy, and Monitoring | Explain that CivicActions owns data on its systems and may monitor activity; articulate that there is no expectation of privacy on company resources | All Staff |
| AUP | Authorized and Personal Use | Distinguish authorized business use from permitted limited personal use; identify the boundaries (lawful, no interference, no unreasonable cost) | All Staff |
| AUP | Prohibited Activities | Identify at least five categories of prohibited behavior (illegal content, unapproved software, credential sharing, circumventing controls, data exfiltration) and explain why each is prohibited | All Staff |
| AUP | Devices — Company-Managed | List the baseline controls on managed laptops (FDE, EDR, firewall, patching, auto-lock) and explain why settings must not be altered | All Staff |
| AUP | Devices — BYOD | State the BYOD restrictions (no Confidential/CUI data), list the four minimum config requirements (screen lock, current OS, encryption, password manager), and describe when to report loss/theft | All Staff |
| AUP | Home and Travel Workspaces | Apply workspace security practices: use WPA2/WPA3, lock screen, protect privacy in shared spaces, minimize printing, close privileged sessions when done | All Staff |
| AUP | Data Handling and Sharing | Classify data by sensitivity level and choose the correct sharing method (named sharing, approved channels only for Confidential/CUI); explain why "anyone with the link" is prohibited for non-Public data | All Staff |
| AUP | Cloud, Social Media, and External Communications | Identify approved vs. unapproved cloud services; state the rules for social media (Public info only, no speaking for company without authorization, no client disclosures) | All Staff |
| AUP | Software Installation and Open-Source Use | Describe the process for requesting new software; identify the Prohibited Hardware and Software list; explain developer obligations for open-source (SBOM, license review, no secrets in code) | All Staff; Developers (deeper) |
| AUP | Development, Automation, and Infrastructure | Use service identities (not personal accounts) for CI/CD; keep privileges minimal; avoid direct production changes; route changes through peer-reviewed pipelines | Developers |
| AUP | Generative AI and Code Assistants | Locate the AI Usage Policy; identify what data may and may not be entered into AI tools | All Staff; Developers (deeper) |
| AUP | Session Management and Authentication | Protect accounts with MFA and password managers; lock/log out when leaving devices; keep high-risk sessions short-lived | All Staff |
| AUP | Malware Protection and Downloads | Cooperate with endpoint protections; scan external files; never disable security agents; download only from trusted sources | All Staff |
| AUP | Reporting Obligations | Recognize reportable events (lost device, credential compromise, mis-sent data, suspicious email, malware); report immediately to security@civicactions.com; describe blame-free reporting culture | All Staff |
| AUP | Consequences for Violations | Understand the range of disciplinary actions (coaching through termination) and that sanctions are proportionate | All Staff |
| AUP | Acknowledgment | Complete the policy acknowledgment at onboarding and after material changes | All Staff |
