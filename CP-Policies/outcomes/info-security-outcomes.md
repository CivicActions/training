# Information Security Policy — Training Outcomes

Source: [Information Security Policy](../policies/Information-Security-Policy_703725569.md)

| Policy | Section | Learning Outcome | Audience |
|--------|---------|-----------------|----------|
| ISP | Governance and Leadership Commitment | Understand that this policy is the umbrella for all security policies; it establishes CISO authority and mandates compliance by all team members and partners; CivicActions operates an ISMS guided by ISO 27001 and NIST CSF 2.0 | All Staff |
| ISP | Security Objectives | State at least three of CivicActions' security objectives: safeguard FCI/CUI per FAR/NIST/CMMC, protect PII/PHI, maintain system integrity and availability, enforce least privilege, and continuously improve the ISMS | All Staff |
| ISP | Core Security Principles | Explain the seven core principles: security by design, least privilege, defense-in-depth, data classification and minimization, openness with safeguards, managed endpoints first, and partner trust-but-verify | All Staff |
| ISP | Compliance Commitments | Identify the compliance frameworks CivicActions aligns to (FAR 52.204-21/CMMC L1, NIST 800-171/CMMC L2, ISO 27001, ISO 9001, ITIL); understand the commitment to maintain audit-ready evidence | All Staff |
| ISP | Delegation to Subordinate Policies | Locate the specific subordinate policy for a given topic (e.g., Acceptable Use for end-user rules, Data Security for classification, Incident Response for reporting); understand the policy hierarchy | All Staff |
| ISP | Risk Management | Explain that CivicActions uses NIST SP 800-30 and NIST CSF 2.0 for risk management; understand that risk acceptance must be explicit, recorded, and approved at the appropriate authority level | All Staff; System/Data Owners (deeper) |
| ISP | Assets, Endpoints, and Remote-First Security | Restate the managed-device requirement for CivicActions work; explain that endpoints must meet baseline controls (FDE, patching, anti-malware, firewall, auto-lock); understand zero-trust remote access principles | All Staff |
| ISP | Secure Engineering, DevOps, and SRE | Explain shift-left security: controls integrated into CI/CD, pre-approved libraries for authn/crypto, automated baseline enforcement; design for least privilege, zero trust, rapid rollback | Developers |
| ISP | Monitoring, Detection, and Incident Response | Understand that services must emit logs and events for security operations; report suspected incidents immediately per the Incident Response Policy | All Staff |
| ISP | Data Governance, Openness, and Classification | Classify data before sharing, publishing, or processing outside CivicActions; public release requires explicit Public classification and vetting; CUI/FCI must remain within approved boundaries | All Staff |
| ISP | Supplier and Third-Party Management | Understand that partners with access to CivicActions data must meet baseline safeguards, accept contractual security obligations, and support assessments per the Third-Party Management Policy | All Staff |
