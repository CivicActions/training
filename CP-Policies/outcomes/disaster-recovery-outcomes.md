# Disaster Recovery Plan — Training Outcomes

Source: [Disaster Recovery Plan](../policies/Disaster-Recovery-Plan_915963911.md)

| Policy | Section | Learning Outcome | Audience |
|--------|---------|-----------------|----------|
| DRP | Policy Awareness | Know that the Disaster Recovery Plan exists and is managed by IT; recovery strategy relies on SaaS provider redundancy and automated failover; Google Workspace (SSO/identity) is the top recovery priority; if a service is unavailable or you suspect an outage, raise a flag in Slack #general, DM an IT team member or your manager, or email security@civicactions.com | All Staff |
| DRP | Communication Plan | Use the communication hierarchy during a DR event: Primary (Slack chat, Zoom video), Secondary (Google Chat), Tertiary (Slack video, Zoom chat); understand that clients/partners are notified only if deliverables are impacted, coordinated by leadership | All Staff |
| DRP | System-Specific Recovery | Know the primary and fallback communication channels for each critical system (Google Workspace, Slack, Zoom, Kandji/Iru, Atlassian, Unanet, GitHub, GitLab, Rippling, Culture Amp); identify the organization actions for each during an outage | IT / Service Desk; System/Data Owners |
| DRP | Backup and Data Protection | Understand that primary backups are managed by SaaS providers; ensure administrative access is limited and documented; use export/backup features where available; do not depend on a single platform for critical documentation | IT / Service Desk; System/Data Owners |
| DRP | Testing and Maintenance | Understand that the DRP is reviewed annually, vendor DR assurances are reviewed annually, and tabletop exercises are conducted as appropriate | IT / Service Desk; System/Data Owners |
| DRP | Post-Incident Review | Document timeline and impact after a significant incident; review response effectiveness; identify improvement actions; track through the continual improvement process | IT / Service Desk; System/Data Owners |
