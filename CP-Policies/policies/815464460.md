# Purpose

CivicActions invests in the growth of its people because learning improves personal development, strengthens our services, and advances our mission. This policy establishes the framework for professional development across CivicActions, combining self-directed learning with company-directed training. It sets governance, roles, and minimum expectations so that development is supported, auditable, and aligned with our Quality Management System (QMS) and policy landscape.

# Scope

This policy applies to all CivicActions Team Members, contractors, and interns. Training and development requirements are scoped by audience and role. Client or project requirements, when stricter, take precedence for covered personnel.

# Roles and Responsibilities

- **Director of People (Policy Owner):** Maintains this policy; ensures resourcing for learning and development operations and tools; coordinates improvement work and audits.
- **COO and CTO (Approvers):** Approve this policy and material updates; ensure alignment with strategy and delivery needs.
- **PeopleOps (Owning Department):** Operates the LMS of record; assigns and tracks mandatory training; provides development guidance and tools; compiles completion reporting; partners with Finance on reimbursement procedures; maintains procedures and playbooks.
- **Compliance:** Owns the improvement register; monitors evidence for audits; coordinates internal audits; ensures policy mapping, version control, and Controlled Document workflow.
- **Practice Leads:** Curate role-based training, certification roadmaps, and recommended resources; may sponsor ProDev sprints and maintain lightweight charters for communities of practice.
- **Managers:** Support development planning; align learning time with project realities; approve use of ProDev resources per procedure; monitor their team's mandatory training status and address exceptions.
- **Team Members:** Own their growth; plan and use learning time responsibly; complete mandatory training by due dates; keep profiles and qualifications current.

# Definitions

- **Professional Development (ProDev):** Planned learning that advances a person's capabilities, credentials, or career path, whether technical, professional, or human skills.
- **Mandatory training:** Company-directed learning that must be completed to meet organizational policy, QMS, or contractual requirements.
- **Role-based training:** Learning assigned to a defined job family, practice, or project role.
- **Learning Management System (LMS):** System of record for enrollment, assignments, reminders, acknowledgments, and completion data. CivicActions uses Rippling for core company training; client systems or Skills Base may be used for project-specific qualifications.
- **Knowledge check:** A scored assessment or equivalent demonstration required to complete a course.
- **ProDev sprint:** A time-boxed, peer-supported cohort for focused learning with demo and retrospective artifacts.
- **Mentor / coach:** An internal or external person who provides structured guidance toward specific development goals.
- **Practice Areas:** CivicActions craft-based communities that curate skills, share knowledge, and steward role-based guidance.

# Policy

### Two-track professional development model

CivicActions uses a two-track model to support both personal growth and organizational readiness.

##### Self-directed professional development

- **Access and eligibility:** All Team Members are encouraged to pursue self-directed learning appropriate to their goals and assignments.
- **Time for learning:** Time for ProDev is planned between the Team Member and their manager (for example, quarterly), balancing client commitments and billable goals for delivery team members. Managers help protect focused learning time, communicate availability to project teams, and encourage intentional planning.
- **Budget and reimbursement:** Budget amounts, eligibility rules, spend approvals, and expense submission windows are set in the **ProDev Budget &amp; Reimbursement Procedure** and may be updated periodically without changing this policy.
- **Allowable uses:** Allowable ProDev expenses include certifications and renewals, courses, books, conference fees, language training, coaching, micro-credentials, accessibility supports such as captioning, and similar learning resources. Hardware purchases are excluded , with a single exception: up to the cost of one current-generation, minimum-spec Raspberry Pi board sold by Adafruit, once every five years, to support hands-on systems learning.
- **No carryover:** Budgets are annual and do not carry over. Exceptions for multi-term programs may be approved by the Executive Team (OR-40+), consistent with the procedure.
- **Manager dialog:** Managers are encouraged to discuss goals and large purchases with team members and to ensure alignment with learning plans. Managers can also help align project assignments with team member goals.

##### Structured training (company-wide and role-based)

- **Company-wide training:** All personnel complete assigned company-wide training as scheduled in the LMS. A critical set is due within 7 days of start; all other onboarding courses are due within 60 days unless otherwise specified. Refresher cadence is set in the course catalog maintained under the Training Policy.
- **Role-based training:** Role-based training is curated by Practice Leads and assigned through the LMS or client systems. When a client provides its own training system, that system is the system of record for those assignments.
- **Knowledge checks and completion:** Courses that require a knowledge check must be passed to complete. Retakes are permitted.
- **Records and evidence:**
    - Employees: Completion and acknowledgments are recorded in Rippling.
    - Contractors: Evidence is collected either from the client system of record or via attestation/records from the contractor's entity when Rippling accounts are not available.
    - Where appropriate, qualifications are also tracked in Skills Base.
    - Records are retained per the Document and Records Control Policy and contract obligations.
- **Accessibility and inclusion:** Training authors must provide reasonable accommodations and accessible materials (for example, captions and transcripts) and avoid including CUI in broadly distributed materials. If project training requires sensitive content, authors must create CUI-safe versions and label access appropriately .
- **Escalation for non-completion:** Managers address missed due dates. Continued non-compliance is escalated to People Operations for resolution consistent with company processes.
- **Delivery operations:** Company-wide trainings follow CivicActions training playbooks and procedures (for example, self-paced modules for onboarding with annual recap segments at AHC). The LMS remains the system of record for assignments and acknowledgments.

### ProDev sprints and Practice Areas

- **ProDev sprints:** Any senior leadership team member (OR-20+) may sponsor voluntary sprints, typically 2-5 hours per week. Sprints include a demo/review and a retrospective with learning artifacts stored centrally. Sprint time is logged to the designated ProDev project/task.
- **Practice Areas:** Meet regularly, and share knowledge through talks, playbooks, and short guides that support role-based capability growth.

### Mentoring and coaching

Managers help Team Members identify mentors or coaches, internally or externally. Participation is encouraged and may be supported by the ProDev budget or built into normal project work as appropriate.

### Measurement, evidence, and continual improvement

- **KPI focus:**
    - CivicActions prioritizes timely completion of mandatory training. The training owner sets the annual target and monitors performance by audience and manager cohort.
- **Evaluation and improvement:** Training programs include simple evaluation plans appropriate to scale and use data from knowledge checks, learner feedback, and manager observations. Findings feed the compliance-owned improvement register and inform content refresh cycles.
- **Internal audits:** Training compliance and evidence are subject to internal audits and management review, with corrective actions tracked through the Quality Management System.

### Data classification for training materials

- Company-wide training materials are generally suitable for public or internal sharing, but authors must prevent inclusion of CUI or other sensitive client information in broadly distributed versions.
- When sensitive content is unavoidable, authors produce a restricted, clearly labeled version and control access accordingly, consistent with CivicActions information security and CUI policies.

### Contractors and partners

- Contractors complete mandatory training relevant to the work they perform. Evidence is collected through the client system of record where available or through partner attestations and stored per the Document and Records Control Policy.
- When external vendors deliver training that handles CivicActions data, normal third-party due diligence applies.

### Budget administration and spending controls

- ProDev budgets are centrally funded and may be adjusted mid-year.
- Over-budget requests require approval by the Executive Team (OR-40+), based on clear organizational need or benefit.
- All spending rules, dollar amounts, eligibility, and documentation are defined in the **ProDev Budget &amp; Reimbursement Procedure** and the **Expenses Procedure** referenced therein.

# Training

- A short overview of this policy is included in onboarding.
- Managers receive guidance on how to monitor their team in Rippling and how to plan learning time with team members.
- Policy updates are announced in #announcements and on the All Humans Call (AHC). Acknowledgment may be collected in the LMS when required.

# Review

- The Sr. Director of People leads an annual review. Material changes require COO and CTO approval.
- The Document Controller tracks status and versions per the Document and Records Control Policy.

# Exceptions

- Exceptions may be approved by the Policy Owner or ELT based on criteria such as client requirements, hardship, or equivalent prior learning.
- All exceptions are documented, time-boxed, and stored with the relevant record (for example, in the LMS or Controlled Document Jira board) per the Document and Records Control Policy.
