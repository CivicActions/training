# Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to establish guidelines for identifying, assessing, contractually control, and continuously monitor risks introduced by third (3rd) parties to CivicActions. CivicActions 3rd parties include:

- Partners (prime and subcontractors)
- Vendors/suppliers
- Contractors
- Cloud service providers (SaaS, bare metal)
- Hardware and software providers
- Professional services
- Software components used for CivicActions and/or its clients

This policy is also referred to as the supply chain risk management (SCRM), cybersecurity supply chain risk management (C-SCRM), vendor risk management, and/or partner management policy.

# Scope

This policy applies to all CivicActions team members involved in selecting, contracting, onboarding, approving, paying for, or working with:

- Digital services (SaaS and cloud) used by CivicActions
- Executable software used on CivicActions managed laptops, in internal systems, or within client delivery (including open-source code and libraries)
- Professional services and subcontractors that support CivicActions projects or operations

# Roles and Responsibilities

- CEO and CTO (Approvers): Approve this policy and exceptions with High residual risk; ensure resources for due diligence and monitoring.
- CISO (Policy Owner): Owns the C-SCRM program, risk tiering, metrics, and integration with the ISMS and Risk &amp; Security Assessment policy; ensures DFARS/CMMC flow-down is enforced in subcontracts when applicable.
- Procurement and Vendor Managers: Operate intake, maintain the partner inventory, coordinate questionnaires and evidence review, and ensure contract clauses reflect supplier risk and flow-down duties.
- System Owners and Data Owners: Define data classification and authorized boundaries; approve access; ensure SaaS and software remain compliant with this policy.
- Engineering (for SCRM-S): Integrate SBOM generation and SCA into CI/CD; feed Dependency-Track; remediate vulnerabilities by policy; participate in the biweekly review.
- Project and Program Managers (for SCRM-PS): Own partner performance and review cadence; confirm training, background checks, and contractual deliverables; escalate issues per this policy.
- Document Controller (Compliance): Controls this Controlled Document and maintains supporting checklists and records per the Document and Records Control process.

# Definitions

- C-SCRM: Cybersecurity supply chain risk management across people, process, technology, and data.
- FCI / CUI: Federal Contract Information and Controlled Unclassified Information as defined by contract and law.
- CIAX: Impact lens for Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability, and eXpense used to tier SaaS risk and review cadence.
- SBOM: Software Bill of Materials; machine-readable inventory of software components.

# Policy

CivicActions organizes supply-chain activities into these three tracks, with detailed procedures maintained for each: SCRM-DS (Digital Services), SCRM-S (Software), and SCRM-PS (Professional Services).

### Governance and Program Structure

1. CivicActions operates a single, organization-wide C-SCRM program with three execution tracks: SCRM-DS, SCRM-S, and SCRM-PS. Each track maintains a procedure with forms, workflows, and acceptance criteria.
2. Supplier risk is identified, analyzed, treated, and monitored through the enterprise Risk &amp; Security Assessment process and recorded in the Risk Register with POA&amp;Ms where needed. Residual High risks require CEO or CTO acceptance.
3. Annual leadership review and SWOT analysis considers supply-chain risks, performance, and regulatory changes; outputs feed improvements to this policy and procedures.

### Risk Tiering and Categorization

CivicActions assigns a **risk tier** at intake that drives due diligence depth, contract clauses, monitoring, and review cadence.

- **Digital services (SaaS):** Inherent risk scored with **CIAX** :
    - C, I, A based on data sensitivity, business process criticality, authn/authz integration, and recoverability
    - X based on annualized spend and switching cost Tiers: **Low / Medium / High** mapped to control depth and review cadence below.
- **Executable software (project and internal):** Risk scored on: data touched, required privileges, update/patch velocity, security posture (SBOM, disclosure policy, signed releases), and vendor trust/community health. Treat FOSS components as suppliers.
- **Professional services (partners/subcontractors):** Risk scored on: access to data and systems, contractual criticality, service continuity, regulatory scope (FCI/CUI), and reliance depth.

### Mandatory Due Diligence Before Use

CivicActions will not procure, deploy, or grant partner access until due diligence is complete for the assigned tier.

**Applies to all suppliers**

- Intake form with business owner, data classification, boundary, and intended use.
- Evidence review based on tier, such as SOC 2/3, ISO/IEC 27001, ISO 9001 certifications, and security whitepapers or audits.
- Standard questionnaires scaled by tier (for example SIG Lite/Core, CSA CAIQ/CCM for cloud).
- Security questionnaire or attestation where external evidence is unavailable; High must provide third-party evidence or accept compensating controls with executive approval.

**Digital services (SCRM-DS)**

- CIAX scoring determines "fast path" (Low/Medium) versus "full review" (High).
- Identity and access model validated (SSO/MFA), data region and encryption reviewed, and exit strategy defined.
- Google Workspace OAuth app registrations are monitored to detect unapproved services; any discovered use is triaged and either onboarded through this process or blocked.
- For services that may touch CUI, FedRAMP Moderate authorization or documented equivalency is required before use.

**Executable software (SCRM-S)**

- SBOM required at intake for packaged software; for libraries and project dependencies the CI/CD pipeline must generate SBOMs (SPDX or CycloneDX), feed Dependency-Track, and ensure remediation of Critical and High issues prior to release.
- Libraries and tools must have an active maintenance posture; any component without updates for more than a year must be risk-assessed and replaced or isolated unless an approved exception exists.
- For bespoke partner code or managed configurations, partners must use secure development practices and peer review; automated SAST/SCA and IaC checks are expected for High tiers.

**Professional services (SCRM-PS)**

- Partner criteria include legal entity and ownership review, staffing background checks appropriate to role, references, relevant certifications, ability to meet data handling restrictions, and acceptance of CivicActions security clauses.
- Where partners will process FCI/CUI, CivicActions verifies required **CMMC level** and any applicable assessment obligations prior to award and flows down the same to sub-tier suppliers.

### Contracting and Required Security Clauses

All supplier relationships must be governed by written agreements that reflect the assigned tier and include, at minimum, the following where applicable:

- **Baseline obligations:** Confidentiality; least-privilege access; encryption in transit and at rest; MFA for administrative access; secure software development and change controls.
- **Right to audit and evidence:** CivicActions may request and review SOC reports, ISO certificates, penetration test summaries, SBOMs, and mitigation status.
- **Incident reporting and cooperation:** Vendor notifies CivicActions **immediately and no later than 24 hours** after discovery of a suspected or confirmed security incident impacting CivicActions data or services, and cooperates with investigation and recovery per the Incident Response Plan.
- **Federal flow-down (when applicable):**
    - FAR 52.204-21 basic safeguarding for covered contractor systems handling FCI.
    - DFARS 252.204-7012 and NIST SP 800-171 for CUI, including 72-hour DoD reporting and media protection; verification of **DFARS 252.204-7019** assessment status in SPRS; and FedRAMP Moderate authorization or equivalency for any CSP that stores, processes, or transmits CUI.
    - Clear "shared responsibility" and SLA terms for external service providers that protect CUI boundaries.
- **Change notification:** Supplier must notify CivicActions of material changes that may affect security or continuity (ownership changes, data location changes, control failures, critical vulnerabilities, service deprecations) and support reassessment.
- **Exit and data return/destruction:** On termination, supplier revokes access, returns or certifies destruction of CivicActions data, and provides artifacts of sanitization if requested.

### Monitoring, Reviews, and Continuous Improvement

Monitoring and review depth aligns to tier and track.

- **Digital services (SaaS):**
    - **Low/Medium:** Annual review of access, CIAX changes, and shared responsibility alignment.
    - **High:** Semi-annual review, SLO/error budget health for services, and verification of incident and change notices.
    - OAuth and DLP telemetry are monitored to detect risky behaviors and unapproved integrations; remediation is tracked.
- **Executable software:**
    - CI/CD policy ensures SBOM refresh at least each release or quarterly, whichever comes first; Dependency-Track alerts are triaged; unresolved Critical/High items open POA&amp;Ms.
    - Portfolio audit flags components with no updates for more than a year for replacement or isolation.
- **Professional services:**
    - Annual performance and compliance review led by program managers with Compliance participation; issues create POA&amp;Ms; SWOT feeds leadership's management review.
- **All suppliers:** Triggers for ad-hoc reassessment include incidents, major service changes, new data classes, scope creep into FCI/CUI, or findings from internal audits.

### Special Requirements for Open-Source Software (FOSS)

FOSS libraries and containers are treated as suppliers.

- SBOM generation is required; known vulnerabilities must be triaged and remediated based on severity; license compliance is verified.
- Selection considers maintainer reputation, release cadence, deprecation risk, responsible disclosure process, signing, and community health.
- High-risk or unmaintained components require isolation, compensating controls, or replacement.

### Prohibited and Restricted Technologies

CivicActions adheres to government advisories and internal prohibitions for specific hardware, software, and suppliers. Procurement must screen against the prohibited technology list and DFARS-referenced restrictions as part of intake and annual reviews.

### Access and Identity Alignment

Partners that access CivicActions systems must use SSO where available and enforce MFA for all administrative access. Shared accounts are prohibited. Non-person identities must be unique, scoped, and rotated per CivicActions standards.

### Integration with Incident Response and Contingency

Supplier incidents or outages that may affect CivicActions data or services are escalated to CivicActions Incident Response; recovery responsibilities follow the shared responsibility model and contract SLAs. Contingency planning ensures continuity for critical partner services, with tested RTO/RPO targets where appropriate.

### Records and Evidence

Procurement and Compliance retain intake forms, tiering decisions, questionnaires and artifacts, contract security clauses, review results, POA&amp;Ms, and exit attestations as Controlled Records consistent with CivicActions' quality and records requirements.

## Requirements by Track (summary)

**SCRM-DS (Digital Services)**

1. Intake with CIAX score; determine fast path vs full review.
2. Verify SSO/MFA, data location, encryption, SLOs, exit plan, and FedRAMP status if CUI may be present.
3. Contract must include incident reporting, audit rights, and shared responsibility.
4. Monitoring: OAuth/DLP telemetry, access review, and CIAX-based cadence.

**SCRM-S (Executable Software)**

1. SBOM and SCA required; pipeline-enforced checks before release; record in Dependency-Track.
2. For partner-delivered code, require peer review, SAST/IaC policy checks, and signed artifacts for High.
3. Annual portfolio audit; remove or isolate unmaintained or unsigned components.

**SCRM-PS (Professional Services)**

1. Partner criteria, certifications where applicable, verification of CMMC level for FCI/CUI work, and DFARS flow-down in subcontracts.
2. PM-led performance and compliance reviews; issues tracked to POA&amp;M; escalations follow IR and contract remedies.

# Training

- At onboarding and annually, Procurement, PMs, Engineering, and relevant staff complete training on this policy, intake workflows, evidence expectations, SBOM/SCA, and contract security clauses. Training content is updated after material regulatory or process changes.

# Review

- This policy is reviewed at least annually by the CISO and Document Controller, and upon significant legal, technological, or organizational changes. Reviews consider audit results, incident trends, and SWOT inputs. Approvals are recorded per the Controlled Documents process.

# Exceptions

- Exceptions are rare and time-boxed. They require a documented risk assessment, compensating controls, and CISO approval; High residual risk requires CEO or CTO approval. Exceptions are recorded in the Risk Register and reviewed at least quarterly.

### Notes for Implementation (non-normative, pointers to procedures)

- **Forms and matrices:** Use the New Services intake form (SCRM-DS) and the Software Evaluation Matrix (SCRM-S).
- **Templates:** Use standard security clauses and the CMMC/DFARS flow-down addendum for subcontracts; include incident notification, right-to-audit, data return/destruction, and shared responsibility.
- **Tooling:** SBOM generation with Syft or Trivy and tracking in Dependency-Track for software; OAuth app monitoring and DLP for SaaS.

This policy meshes with CivicActions' Information Security, Data Security and Handling, Risk &amp; Security Assessment, Incident Response, and Training policies and delegates operational detail to the SCRM-DS, SCRM-S, and SCRM-PS procedures to avoid overlap and confusion.
