## **Purpose**

The purpose of this policy is to establish guidelines for CivicActions employees regarding the responsible and secure handling of Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI). This policy aims to prevent the unauthorized disclosure of CUI and provides a default overview of sensitive information types and methods of special protection and handling when not covered by an Originating Agency's CUI policies.

## **Scope**

This policy applies to all CivicActions Team Members and Contractors who may create or receive access to documents containing Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI) in the course of their work.

## **Definitions**

- Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI): Information that the government owns or uses, or that someone creates for the government, which isn't secret but still needs to be protected. There are rules about how to keep this information safe and who is allowed to see it. Examples of CUI we are likely to come across include:
    - Cybersecurity information related to system or network vulnerabilities, such as system inventories and application vulnerability scan results.
    - Contractor bids, proposals, estimates, modifications, and other non-public contract data (some FCI).
    - For a full list, refer to the CUI Categories on the National Archives website.

- Sensitive Information: Includes various types of information requiring special handling and protection, such as Confidential Information, Federal Contract Information (FCI), and previously termed "Sensitive but Unclassified (SBU)" or "For Official Use Only (FOUO)" information, now largely replaced by CUI.
- CivicActions Workstation: A client-provided Government Furnished Equipment (GFE), a CivicActions furnished managed device, or with CISO or CTO permission, an appropriately hardened personal (BYOD) device.
- CivicActions CUI Security Boundary: CUI must be stored on or accessed from controlled access environments, including:
    - A secured network provided and maintained by a client agency/organization.
    - The CivicActions Google Workspace, including internal email and access-controlled, client-specific or sales/procurement Google Shared Drives marked "CUI".
        - Note that the CivicActions Google Workspace will auto-tag any document containing the words "Controlled Unclassified Information" or "CUI" as "Confidential".
    - A CivicActions Workstation.

## **Roles and Responsibilities**

- Chief Technology Officer (CTO): Oversees company technology policies.
- Chief Information Security Officer (CISO): Oversees overall compliance with this policy.
- Managers: Responsible for ensuring team awareness, policy compliance, and proper training related to CUI handling.
- Team Members: Responsible for handling sensitive information in a secure manner as outlined by this policy.

## **Policy**

### **Overview**

CivicActions team members may interact with sensitive information, including CUI, on a "need-to-know" basis or when securely connected to an agency network via a CivicActions Workstation. This policy outlines the mandatory rules and principles for safeguarding CUI to prevent unauthorized disclosure.

### **Requirements**

While this document primarily pertains to CUI, other sensitive information types may require special handling. If unsure, consult with your manager, the CISO or the CTO.

### **Safeguarding CUI**

- Take necessary steps to reasonably ensure unauthorized individuals cannot access or observe CUI.
- Protect the confidentiality of CUI that is processed, stored, or transmitted on Federal information systems or limited access CivicActions systems and workstations.
- If requirements or sensitivity are unclear, contact the company CISO, CTO, ask on the #loving-security Slack channel or email [security@civicactions.com](mailto:security@civicactions.com) .

### **CUI Handling Guidelines**

- CUI documents shall not be transferred, printed, or saved to a non-secure device outside the CivicActions CUI Security Boundary
- If a copy of CUI is no longer needed, it must be deleted as soon as possible. The original may need to be maintained for National Archives FOIA requests.
    - Project closeout procedures should include an audit of all project CUI, transfer to the client if needed, then deletion from CivicActions systems at the end of the project.
- The suspected compromise or unauthorized access of CUI must be reported as a Security Incident following Agency Practices with a fallback to the CivicActions [Incident Response Policy](https://www.google.com/search?q=https://guidebook.civicactions.com/en/latest/common-practices-tools/security/incidents/%23reporting-an-incident) .

### **CUI Document Markings**

Documents created and accessed solely within an Agency's security boundary or within the CivicActions CUI Security Boundary may not require marking. However, if a document containing CUI is not marked and is to be shared outside its security boundary, it must be marked as follows:

- "CONTROLLED UNCLASSIFIED INFORMATION" or "CUI" in the header or top of each page.
- At the bottom of the first page, add: **Sensitive in accordance with 32 CFR 2002** (bold and centered).
- Optionally, per Agency: on the next line, add: Controlled by: [Originating Agency], [POC], [Originator's First, Middle Last Name].

## **Training**

This policy will be communicated via internal updates and annual training sessions. Any employee who may come in contact with CUI needs to register for at least one CUI training per year (any Agency Document Management or CUI training will meet this requirement).

## **Review**

This document will be reviewed annually by the CISO, with additional reviews following significant technological or regulatory changes. Changes require CISO approval, supported by legal or expert review as necessary.

## **Exceptions**

All exception requests must be submitted to the CISO for approval. Exceptions will be documented and reviewed annually for continued validity.
