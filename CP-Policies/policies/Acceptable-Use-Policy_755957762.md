# Purpose

The purpose of the Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) is to set clear expectations and requirements for how CivicActions team members use company and client IT resources. The AUP connects CivicActions security and compliance commitments to day-to-day expectations for a remote-first workforce, enabling team members to do great work while protecting our clients, colleagues, and organizational reputation. This policy defines expected behaviors that complement technical safeguards on company-managed devices, personal devices used for CivicActions work, and cloud services.

# Scope

This policy applies to all CivicActions Team Members, contractors, interns, and third parties who access CivicActions or client systems or data. It covers:

- Company-managed laptops and mobile devices, client-furnished systems, and any personal devices used for CivicActions work
- CivicActions networks and all approved cloud and SaaS services (for example, Google Workspace, Slack, Zoom, Atlassian, Rippling, Unanet, GitHub/GitLab)
- All information we create, store, process, or transmit, including FCI, CUI, PII, PHI, and CivicActions internal information

# Roles and Responsibilities

- CEO and CTO (Approvers): Approve this policy and material revisions

# Definitions

- Managed device: A company-managed endpoint under MDM with enforced baseline controls
- BYOD: Personally owned device used for CivicActions work under the conditions in this policy
- Approved service: A cloud or SaaS service that has passed CivicActions intake and is listed as approved for use
- Authorized use: Usage aligned with your job responsibilities, or as otherwise approved by policy or management
- Confidential information: Company or client data labeled Internal, Confidential, Restricted, or CUI/FCI under the Data Security and Handling Policy
- CUI and FCI: Controlled Unclassified Information and Federal Contract Information as defined by contract and regulation

# Policy

## Principles for Acceptable Use

CivicActions expects all Team Members to:

- Use only approved devices and services to do CivicActions work
- Protect accounts and data, follow least privilege , and limit use to authorized purposes
- Prefer company-managed devices for anything beyond public information
- Treat sensitive information with care and minimize copies and exposure
- Choose open and collaborative tools when information is public or internal , and protected channels when it is not
- When in doubt, ask for approval rather than bypass controls
- Client Acceptable Use policies and/or Rules of Behavior must be followed whenever client systems or data is involved

## Ownership, Privacy, and Monitoring

### CivicActions Services

CivicActions systems, accounts, and data that is created, sent, received, or stored on CivicActions-owned or administered resources are CivicActions property. Team Members should have no expectation of privacy when using CivicActions IT resources. CivicActions may monitor, log, and review activity to maintain security, fulfill legal obligations, investigate incidents, and ensure policy compliance. Use indicates consent to monitoring which may occur with or without advance notice, subject to applicable law or due to technical requirements.

### CivicActions Laptops

All CivicActions-related content sent, received, or stored on CivicActions laptops and other managed devices is the property of CivicActions. While CivicActions permits occasional personal use of company devices, team members shouldn't have any expectation of privacy in connection with any information sent, received, or stored on the device. CivicActions may monitor or access information stored on the device (and in fact management and maintenance of device security typically requires accessing some information on a routine basis). Such monitoring also may include CivicAction's incidental viewing of personal communication or information the team member stores on or sends using the device. Therefore we recommend that you be mindful of using the device for personal reasons.

## Authorized and Personal Use

CivicActions resources are for business use. Limited personal use of CivicActions Laptops is permitted when it is lawfu l, does not interfere with work, does not incur unreasonable cost or load, and follows this policy and other CivicActions policies.

## Prohibited Activities

The following are not allowed when using CivicActions or client systems, networks, or data:

- Accessing, creating, storing, or transmitting illegal content, hate or harassment, or material that violates contract terms or platform acceptable use rules
- Installing, running, or distributing unapproved or prohibited software or hardware; using consumer "optimizer," "free VPN," ad-ware, or similar risky tools; using any software on t he [CivicActions Prohibited Hardware and Software list](https://guidebook.civicactions.com/en/latest/company-policies/prohibited-hardware-and-software/) (for example, Huawei/ZTE devices, TikTok, Kaspersky) for CivicActions work or to access CivicActions resources
- Sharing accounts or credentials, storing passwords outside approved password managers , or using another person's identity or session
- Circumventing security controls, disabling device management/monitoring tools or encryption, jailbreaking/rooting devices used for work, using non-approved VPNs or tunnel tools
- Exfiltrating or exposing data, including posting internal, Confidential, FCI, or CUI information to public sites, forums, social media, or unapproved generative AI tools ; emailing such data to personal accounts; or syncing it to personal cloud storage
- Automatic forwarding of CivicActions email to an outside account (e.g. Slack or a personal [gmail.com](http://gmail.com/) account) is prohibited
- Using unapproved external systems to store or process CivicActions or client data; connecting CivicActions systems to external services without approval
- Performing scanning, scraping, crypto-mining, bulk automation, or API activity that degrades service or violates platform terms; overwhelming internal or third-party infrastructure with automated requests
- Conducting penetration testing, social engineering, or security research on CivicActions or client systems without written authorization
- Bypassing project boundaries or accessing data beyond the job role

## Devices and Endpoints

### Company-Managed Devices

- Company-managed laptops are required for access to Internal, Confidential, Restricted, FCI, or CUI data. Baseline controls include full-disk encryption, EDR, host firewall, automatic patching, and auto-lock. Device security settings must not be altered without authorization.

### Bring-Your-Own Device (BYOD)  (personally owned devices)

- BYOD, or personally-owned devices, such as laptops or desktops must not be used to access or store Confidential or Restricted data, such as FCI or CUI. Limited exceptions require prior written approval and equivalent controls.
- BYOD mobile devices may be used for MFA and communications only. Avoid downloading documents to mobile storage. If enrolled under a managed profile, users consent to required security settings and remote wipe of work data.
- Minimum BYOD configuration:
    - Screen locks enabled and set to lock after 20 minutes of inactivity
    - Current OS, security patches, and vendor-supported device models
    - Device encryption enabled; no jailbroken or rooted devices
    - Approved password manager for any residual app credentials
- Report loss, theft, or compromise immediately.
- These conditions are required to protect CivicActions data while recognizing the limits of BYOD.

## Home and Travel Workspaces

Follow the CivicActions [Work Location Policy](https://civicactions.atlassian.net/wiki/x/AgBkI) for specific requirements on work from home, local locations, work locations outside local municipality, and working outside of the state or country.

Additional requirements includes:

- Use WPA2 or WPA3 Wi-Fi or wired Ethernet. Do not use open Wi-Fi for work. Change default router admin passwords and keep router firmware updated.
- Protect privacy in shared spaces. Lock your screen when away. Avoid discussing or displaying sensitive information where others can overhear or view.
- Minimize printing, especially of sensitive information. If printing sensitive information is required, secure output and shred promptly using a cross-cut shredder.
- Close privileged sessions and tabs to admin consoles, finance systems, CI/CD, or cloud management when work is complete. Do not keep elevated sessions open in the background.

## Data Handling and Sharing

- Follow CivicActions classification and handling rules. Share Internal, Confidential, Restricted, FCI, and CUI through approved locations only. Use named sharing in Google Drive. Do not use "anyone with the link" for non-Public data.
- Only send Confidential, Restricted, FCI, and CUI by authorized channels.
- CUI and FCI must be marked and handled per contract and rules. Users are responsible for marking CUI and using only authorized CUI boundaries.

## Cloud, Social Media, and External Communications

- Use only approved cloud services for CivicActions work. Your use must also comply with the provider's acceptable use policy.
- Do not store CivicActions or client information in personal cloud accounts (for example, personal Gmail, Dropbox, iCloud).
- Social media and public forums:
    - Share only Public information
    - Do not speak on behalf of CivicActions unless authorized
    - Do not disclose client relationships, non-public deliverables, or internal operations
- Business texting, conferencing, and collaboration must use approved tools unless an exception is granted.

## Software Installation and Open-Source Use

- Install only software that is approved and required for your role. Requests for new tools follow the Access and Authorization process. Unauthorized software is prohibited.
- Developers may use open-source components consistent with CivicActions Engineering Security and Compliance guidance, including SBOM/SCA, vulnerability remediation, license review, no secrets in code, and use of approved secret managers.
- Prohibited or restricted technologies listed in [CivicActions' Prohibited Hardware and Software Standard](https://guidebook.civicactions.com/en/latest/company-policies/prohibited-hardware-and-software/) must not be used for CivicActions purposes.

## Development, Automation, and Infrastructure

- Use service or workload identities for automation and CI/CD, not personal accounts. Keep privileges minimal and scoped to the task. Protect tokens and API keys in approved secret stores.
- Do not perform direct manual changes in production or other controlled environments. Infrastructure changes must go through version-controlled, peer-reviewed pipelines.
- Do not overwhelm internal or external platforms with excessive automated requests or abusive scripts.

## Generative AI and code assistants

Review the CivicActions [AI Usage Policy](https://civicactions.atlassian.net/wiki/x/AwC3Ig) to see specific guidelines and requirements on how CivicActions is allowed ot use AI.

## Session Management and Authentication

- Protect accounts with MFA and password managers as required by the Identification Policy. Never share authenticators, recovery codes or allow any unexpected approval requests via mobile pop-ups .
- Lock or log out when leaving a device unattended. Sessions to high-risk systems or accounts must be short-lived and closed when not in use.

## Malware Protection and Downloads

- Scan files from external sources, cooperate with endpoint protections, and do not disable security agents. Only download from trusted sources .

## Reporting Obligations

- Report immediately: lost or stolen devices, suspected credential compromise, mis-sent or exposed data, suspicious email or downloads, malware warnings, or any suspected policy violation. Reports go to [security@civicactions.com](mailto:security@civicactions.com) - see Incident Response Pla n for more details. Prompt reporting is expected and blame-free.

## Consequences for Violations

- Violations may result in actions including coaching, mandatory retraining, removal of access, written warning, suspension, termination of employment or contract, and legal action if required. Contractual remedies may apply for partners and suppliers. Disciplinary processes are used as a deterrent and are proportionate to the issue.
