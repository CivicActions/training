## Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to establish guidelines for the responsible, secure, and compliant use of artificial intelligence (AI) tools and models within CivicActions.

## Scope

This policy applies to all CivicActions employees, contractors, and third parties involved in the selection, deployment, usage, or management of AI systems and tools, including generative and other specialized AI systems pursuant to their work for or with CivicActions.

## Definitions

- **AI** or **Artificial Intelligence** : Computer systems capable of performing tasks that typically require human-like intelligence, such as learning, problem-solving, and decision-making
- **Generative AI** : Pre-trained AI models that generate new text, images, or other media from user inputs or prompts.
- **Sensitive Information** : Includes Personal Identifiable Information (PII), Personal Health Information (PHI), Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI), client proprietary data, and CivicActions confidential data (legal, financial, contractual, or human resources).

## Roles and Responsibilities

- **Chief Technology Officer (CTO)** : Approves exceptions and oversees overall compliance.
- **CivicActions IT** : Manages approved tools, grants and revokes licenses, and assesses new internal AI tools.
- **Team Members** : Adhere to policy guidelines, request licenses via IT, and seek peer reviews for AI-generated outputs.
- **Managers** : Ensure team awareness, policy compliance, and proper training.

## Policy

### Overview

Artificial Intelligence (AI) offers CivicActions significant opportunities for enhancing productivity, innovation, and service delivery, such as improved efficiency in software development, data analysis, content creation, and problem-solving. However, AI usage also introduces risks, including confidentiality breaches, data privacy violations, misinformation, bias, security vulnerabilities, intellectual property issues, and trust erosion. This policy ensures AI use is lawful, secure, ethical, transparent, and compliant with applicable standards.

### General Requirements

- Only use CivicActions-approved generative AI tools with accounts managed by IT - or client-approved tools on a project.
- Never input sensitive or confidential information into AI tools.
- Ensure human oversight and accountability for AI-generated content:
    - Verify all AI-generated outputs for accuracy, appropriateness, bias, security risks, intellectual property, and alignment with CivicActions' values and client requirements.
    - Clearly label AI-generated or AI-assisted content internally.
    - Peer review all AI outputs before they are used for a business purpose or shared externally.

### Usage in Software Developmen t

- Use GitHub Copilot exclusively for source code creation.
    - Carefully review and test AI generated code for correctness and security.
    - Describe if/how AI was used in peer review requests.
- Technical queries (problem-solving, brainstorming etc.) can utilize other approved tools provided they don't generate usable source code .

### Usage on Client Projects

- Comply with any client-specific policies and guidance regarding AI usage and disclosure.
    - These should be included in project on-boarding &amp; wrapper documents.
- Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI) should not be entered into AI tools.

### Usage in Sales

- Do not use AI tools for pricing or original RFP content generation.
- AI tools may assist in analysis, review, and content rewriting.

### Approved Generative AI Tools

"License required" below means that a CivicActions-managed license is required - these tools must not be used for work with a personal license.

- **General purpose** :
    - CivicActions Slack Chatbot
    - Google Workspace Gemini &amp; NotebookLM
    - ChatGPT for Teams (license required)
        - ChatGPT's Atlas browser is **not** approved
- **Coding** :
    - GitHub Copilot (license required)
- **Specialist AI tools:**
    - Grammarly (license required)
    - Paxton AI (license required)
- **Service specific generative AI integrations** :
    - Zoom (recording summaries)
    - Mural
    - Confluence/Jira (and associated plugins that use AI, such as Draw.io)
    - Greenhouse
    - Figma
- **Programmatic Services (approval required)** :
    - Google Cloud Vertex AI API
    - Microsoft Azure OpenAI API
- **Single-purpose tools (built into approved IT services)** such as search, spell/grammar check, e-mail filtering, speech recognition, machine translation, optical character recognition.

### AI Usage Approval

Approval by the CTO (and client for client-specific tools) is required for other AI tools or significant new AI applications of existing tools.

Conduct a proportionate risk assessment guided by the NIST AI Risk Management Framework for new AI tools or applications.

### Usage in Professional Developmen t

Team members may freely explore AI tools and topics, provided only publicly available data is used.

## Training

This policy will be communicated via internal updates and annual training sessions.

## Review

This document will be reviewed annually by the CTO and Compliance, with additional reviews following significant technological or regulatory changes. Changes require CTO approval, supported by legal or expert review as necessary.

## Exceptions

All exception requests must be submitted to the CTO for approval. Exceptions will be documented and reviewed annually for continued validity.
